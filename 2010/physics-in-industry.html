Why Physics?

Why Physics?

So why get a physics degree instead of a computer science degree?

Why Physics?

CS teaches how things work and how they have been solved.

It values explanation (what/how) over exploration (why).

Why Physics?

Physics teaches how to derive general solutions to specific problems.

Exploration leads to explanation.

You will learn a lot of math.

An Example

Computer science does not teach variance or error.

Many of them think that a single measurement is a valid source of data. It is common in online literature. As a physicist you will understand statistics in a way that most computer scientists don't.

Another Example

The Program is Slow

How an (average) computer scientist investigates:

  1. Users complain the system is slow.
  2. Programmer mucks with the code a little.
  3. Programmer tests application: it's a bit better.
  4. Programmer remembers something he read online.
  5. Programmer implements new technology: it's a bit better.
  6. Programmer claims the problem is fixed.

After a few rounds of this the code becomes pretty bloated and unmaintainable.

Another Example

The Program is Slow

How an real scientist investigates:

  1. Users complain the system is slow.
  2. Scientist measures the application and sets a threshold for acceptable performance.
  3. Scientist takes measurements to determine the source.
  4. With the worst component identified, the scientist proposes a fix.
  5. The scientist implements the fix and takes new measurements.
  6. If the fix did not improve performance, it is removed, otherwise we go back to step 4.

Code is kept clean, as only that which makes a measurable improvement is used in production.

What about you?

What about you?

So what does this all mean to you? What if you want to pursue a career in technology?

Your Degree is Fine

Don't worry about whether or your degree looks good to employers on paper. They care more about what you have proven you can do than what you might be able to do.

Learn Math

If you are in any vaguely technical field, math is valuable. Computer science was originally a branch of applied mathematics and it still is!

One thing that separates great programmers from good programmers is the ability to reason at higher levels of abstraction and math is nothing but abstractions.

I wish I had paid more attention in my linear algebra class.

Don't Worry About Specifics

Don't invest your time in specific technologies. Invest your time in concepts, ideas, and practices. The computer industry has many fads which come and go. Look for resources that have stood the test of time.

Play to your strengths as a problem solver. Good, analytical thinkers are actually not that common.

Job Hunting is About Numbers

Don't get discouraged by rejections. There are a lot of technology jobs out there and a lot of candidates. It may take a while to find one that fits you.
